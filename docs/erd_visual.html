Design Highlights

  • Idempotent ingestion: billing_records uses ON CONFLICT DO UPDATE (upsert) keyed on (usage_date, subscription_id, resource_group, service_name, meter_category) — safe to re-run without duplicates.
  • Soft-delete by status: anomalies and ingestion_alerts use status/is_active fields rather than physical deletes, preserving audit history.
  • Polymorphic event delivery: notification_deliveries.event_id references alert_events, anomalies, or ingestion_alerts without a FK constraint — event_type discriminates the target table.
  • Point-in-time audit snapshots: alert_events stores budget_amount and threshold_percent at fire-time — budget edits after the fact cannot alter the historical record.
  • No FK on tenant attribution: billing_records.tag → tenant_attributions.tenant_id is a soft string-key join. Tenants are auto-discovered from Azure tags, so referential integrity is enforced at the service layer.
  • 3NF trade-offs (intentional): allocation_rules.manual_pct (JSON avoids child table), notification_channels.config_json (polymorphic channel config), tenant_attributions.top_service_category (denorm for O(1) dashboard reads).
  • UUID primary keys throughout: All 13 tables use UUID PKs — safe for distributed generation and merge-safe across environments.
